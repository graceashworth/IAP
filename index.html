Root cause

My self-worth is closely tied to my performance

Drives my fear of failure, which is masked as anxiety. Manifests not only in my leadership abilities but largely in my personal life.

Brainstorm

How might I address this?

With my root cause identified, these are the possible steps I could take to stop tying my value to what I accomplish

A

Return to therapy

Something I stopped in March when school and work became too busy

  • Provides a dedicated space to work through the root cause directly
  • Gives structure and accountability beyond self-monitoring
  • Can focus specifically on detaching self-worth from performance
B

Schedule and protect personal time each week

Free from work or law school prep, and honored even when anxiety says otherwise

  • Creates a concrete, repeatable boundary between productivity and rest
  • Forces me to practice existing outside of achievement
  • Broad enough to encompass other actions over time
C

Learn to genuinely enjoy free time

Not just "not working" but making that time restorative and mine

  • Getting back into dancing, reading, being fully present
  • Rebuilding an identity outside of academic performance
  • "Strengthening relationships I neglected throughout college

Why I chose B

Option B is broad enough to make A and C possible. Without protected time, I won't return to therapy or learn to rest. It's the structural change that will allow me to start building habits that detach my self-worth from my performance.

My commitment

Action plan

Behavioral commitment

Scheduling and protecting personal time each week that is completely free from work or law school prep, and honoring it even my anxiety tells me productivity/work is urgent.

What this looks like

  • Protecting my Saturdays as personal time, completely free from work and law school prep.
  • In college, my weekends haven't ever truly felt like time I could rest, so now with no academic work to complete I can begin to learn how to actually rest.
  • No emails, no LinkedIn,and no application prep during personal time.
  • Spending time with friends and family, and doing things I genuinely enjoy: reading, getting back into dancing, being fully present in what I am doing.
  • Returning to therapy with a specific focus on learning to detach my self-worth from my performance.

Pros

  • Builds a healthier relationship between rest and my self-worth over time
  • Protects my mental health during a high pressure post-grad transition.
  • Strengthen relationships that have been neglected throughout college.
  • Creates sustainable habits before starting a career and law school.
  • Gives me space to rediscover who I am outside of my academic identity.

Cons

  • May feel uncomfortable or guilt-inducing at first
  • Hard to maintain during high pressure periods like law school application deadlines.
  • Requires saying no to work, even when anxiety is telling me to keep going.
  • Without school as structure, it may be easy to fill personal time with applications without realizing it.

When things get hard

Contingencies

What if law school applications become overwhelming or I need to meet a deadline for them?

If this happens, I'll give myself permission to shift my me day to Sunday rather than give it up entirely over the weekend.

What if I slip back into old patterns?

I will bring this plan to my therapist so they can hold me accountable during our sessions, making it a consistent check-in/discussion point. I will also make a point to have my siblings and mom check in with me about it as well on the off chance I stop theapry for any reason.

What if I miss a week of personal time?

I will not punish myself or use it as evidence that I am failing, instead I'll flag it with my therapist and recommit to my time the following week.

What if work bleeds into my weekends?

I will scale back rather than eliminate completely. Even one hour on a Saturday counts. The goal is consistency over time, not perfection in any single week.
